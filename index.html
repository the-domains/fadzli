What the world really needs..

It’s pretty difficult to pinpoint a time in our past when our parents lost out to innovation… it’s around the time that we started writing testimonials on friendster and later scribbling on each other’s walls on FB. Our parents just didn’t ‘get’ why we would want to open up so much of our lives to ‘strangers’.

Well, these weren’t ‘strangers’ (initially). It was a close group of friends, then friends of friends, then friends of friends of friends, then suddenly it’s random Tom who saw you at an event and bam! he’s one of actual strangers apart from your real up-to-150 friends on facebook.

Now I realise that I, as a parent, am slowly loosing out to innovation. I just don’t ‘get’ snapchat. How does one have soooo much time, and discipline (trust me, they don’t just wake up looking like that), and money (cause if you ain’t working how are you surviving?)

DOPAMINE! That’s how. I grew up in a generation where social association gave you self-worth. Many of us were comparing how many friends we had on which social network. Q in Kim Kardashian, her hot cross buns and every other reality tv star who embodied success without the need of a core talent (although I would argue the core talent is the ability to unrelentingly be a narcissist on camera *not easy*) and the generation between my children and me are… narcissists addicted to self-gratification and viewership.

Over time, we get more disconnected, we stare at phones more than into our loved ones’ eyes. In time the digital hearts, thumbs up and smileys may actually give u a proper orgasm, better than your actual partner.

So what the world really needs is… phones with better battery life. Hahahahaha. to feed this beast of innovation. I ain’t gonna be left behind and finally join the ‘new’ trend 10 years later, like my parents’ generation. Snapchat, Instastories, here I come!

BIGO live, bitches!

ps. Oh ya, and GRID. cause building a site from scratch or a framework is sooo 2016. We got robots/AI/Will Smith’s translucent friend doing that for us now.
